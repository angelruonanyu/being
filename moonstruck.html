Brew & Being
Come for the coffee. Stay for the people. Leave with something that matters.
What this is about

Liam, I'm Angel. I'm building Living Well Initiative — a non-profit platform for people who believe that how we live is the most important work we'll ever do. Not productivity, not optimisation. Actually learn and practice to live well.

I've been admiring your work with Moonstruck for a while. There's something about the space — the way it holds people — that feels like more than just a coffee shop. I wanted to reach out because I think we might be building toward the same thing from different directions.

This is a proposal for a collaboration. Not a big ask — just an idea worth a conversation.

Find out more about what we do: beinghome.uk

What is Brew & Being

A gathering of 10–15 people around coffee, tea, and honest conversation. Just a room of people who want to take life a little more seriously — together.

The evening has three parts: you guide a light-roast coffee tasting. I brew Chinese tea by hand — and share a little about what we're building at Being. Then we eat hand-baked rose Basque cheesecake and talk about what we're carrying, and what we keep forgetting to notice.

What we see in Moonstruck

You're already doing something rare. A few things stand out:

Slow is a choice you've already made.
Light roast, hand pour, single origin. Each step is an act of attention. That is already a showcase in the London cafe scene.
The space holds people differently.
People come to Moonstruck not just for the coffee; they come to slow down and enjoy time. You've built a container for that.
There's a bigger story waiting.
Specialty coffee is a category. "A space that helps you live better" is a movement. I believe Moonstruck is being part of the second — not just a very good coffee shop, but one of London's spaces for intentional living.
How the evening flows
7:00
Doors open
Guests arrive, settle in. Each person gets a small card — write down one thing on your mind tonight. No sharing required.
7:10
Coffee tasting — led by Liam
Light roast, single origin, one pour. Not a class — a conversation about what it takes to make one cup right, and what that attention means.
7:30
A moment of quiet
5 minutes. Intentional. Sit with your cup.
7:35
Chinese tea + Living Well Initiative — Angel
Hand-brewed Chinese tea, and a short introduction to Living Well Initiative — what we're building and why. A conversation about slowness, intention, and the art of living well.
8:20
Rose Basque cheesecake + open conversation
We eat, we talk. Each person shares one thing if they want to. No agenda — just honest conversation.
8:50
Close + linger
A short close. Each person writes one thing they want to carry into the week. Then whoever wants to stay, stays.
